# Line 7 Conveyor Mechanical — Troubleshooting Manual

Covers the belt, drive pulley, and mechanical drivetrain of the Line 7 conveyor.

## CNV-01 — Belt slippage from worn drive pulley

**Severity:** medium

A mismatch between commanded VFD speed and actual measured belt speed, especially accompanied by a rhythmic squeal, points to drive pulley lagging wear. Re-lagging is a planned-maintenance item; in the interim, cleaning and re-tensioning can restore acceptable grip for one to two more shifts.

**Likely causes:**
- Drive pulley lagging worn smooth from years of use
- Belt tension below spec, allowing slip under load
- Contamination (oil/grease) on the pulley surface reducing friction

**Diagnostic steps:**
1. Visually inspect the pulley lagging for shine/smoothness compared to a new reference pulley
2. Measure actual belt speed with a tachometer against the VFD's commanded speed
3. Check for oil or grease contamination near the drive end

**Fix procedure:**
1. Clean the pulley surface with the approved degreaser if contamination is found
2. Re-tension the belt to spec
3. If lagging is worn smooth, schedule pulley re-lagging at next planned downtime

**Reported symptoms typically include:**
- "Belt seems to be slipping on the drive pulley, panels are arriving late to station 3."
- "Conveyor speed looks inconsistent even though the VFD readout is steady."
- "Hearing a rhythmic squeal from the drive end, and the line is running slower than takt."
